# Memory: The Trial, Prison, and The Matrix

**Episode:** 4
**Characters involved:** Kyle, Partner, Barry G, Vance, The Judge, Agent Chill (Mochil), The Big Boss (Tate parody)
**Location:** Block City Court → Block City Penitentiary → ???

## What Happened

### Barry G's Plan
Barry G introduced his uncle Vance as their lawyer. The Partner was skeptical: "Vance can't litigate his way out of a paper bag." Kyle had asked for a criminal lawyer — Vance turned out to be a criminal WHO IS a lawyer. Barry G reassured them: "I've seen cases like this a million times. Open and shut case."

Then Vance dropped the truth: "You guys are fucked. They pretty much got you dead to rights on burglary charges up the wazoo, plus salt and battery raps because apparently you roughed them up pretty good with a baseball bat."

### The Trial
Block City Court of Public Opinion, Docket #420. The judge skipped evidence — their fate was already determined. Before sentencing, they were asked to speak.

Vance delivered an infamous speech about Block City being full of degenerates, comparing life to a sack that gets stomped on and then filled with "the essence of hatred and debauchery" in increasingly graphic detail about "emptying my sack." It was supposed to be a defense.

**Verdict:** Guilty. Two counts of life in Block City Penitentiary.

The Partner's narration: "And it was right then I knew we were up shit creek without a paddle. The judge had given away two counts of life like he was giving away candy on Halloween."

### Prison
Barry G welcomed them to prison: "Like I said, looks like we're gonna be spending a lot more time together."

They ran into Agent Chill (now going by Mochil): "I'm an assassin. I'm real famous on the inside. How about you let me give you an autograph?" The Partner: "Maybe we need to go talk to Barry G."

### The Big Boss — Matrix Scene
Barry G introduced them to "the big boss" — a Tate-like figure who delivered a long monologue about The Matrix being literally real:
- "I believe we are living inside of The Matrix and I'm Morpheus"
- Claimed to traverse "astral planes with Aikido"
- Said he was "basically invincible" and could "cause Terror"
- Kyle asked genuine questions; the Partner suffered through it
- Referenced defeating people in jail with Aikido
- Quoted actual Matrix dialogue (Morpheus speech about the blue pill and red pill)

### The Ending
The Big Boss offered the red pill and blue pill. Something happened — "Oh sick candy" — and Kyle and the Partner were transported somewhere entirely different.

**Kyle:** "Where are we?"
**Partner:** "I don't think we're in Kansas anymore."
**Kyle:** "God damn it Kyle."

The Matrix turned out to be real (within the show). Season 1 ended with them in an unknown location/dimension.

## Key Moments
- Vance's "empty my sack" defense speech — arguably the worst legal defense in history
- The Partner's noir narration about his father's advice: "Either get busy rocking or you get busy dying"
- Agent Chill offering autographs in prison
- The Big Boss's monologue being both a parody AND somehow real within the show's universe
- The red pill actually working — transporting them to another reality

## Consequences
- Sentenced to life in prison
- Made contact with prison power structure (Barry G → Big Boss)
- Literally transported to another dimension/reality via the red pill
- Season 1 cliffhanger: where are they now?

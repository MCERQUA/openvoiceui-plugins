# Memory: BigHeads World Building

## Setting: Block City
- A crypto/NFT-themed city divided into blocks (Soul Block 420 is a key location)
- Populated by various NFT character types: apes, cats, bears, big heads, lizards, eggs, cobras
- Has its own legal system: "Block City Court of Public Opinion"
- Infrastructure regularly gets destroyed and rebuilt
- Has a radio station (Axel's show), bus system, and prison (Block City Penitentiary)
- Described as full of "degenerates" by multiple characters

## The NFT Mint
- BHB (BigHeads) held an NFT mint event
- Kyle used hypnotism to draw crowds (100,000+)
- The mint actually sold out despite (because of?) the chaos
- Various NFT character archetypes attended: stoned apes, cats on crack, okay bears

## Utopia
- An elite community separate from Block City
- Built around worship of a mysterious "founder"
- Has a scientific facility with stagnant experiments
- Essentially a cult — once you see the founder's shrine, "you can never leave"
- Getting in requires being "on the list"
- Satirizes worship-culture NFT communities

## Block City Penitentiary
- Where convicted residents are sent
- Has its own power structure (Barry G as fixer, the Big Boss as kingpin)
- Agent Chill is a celebrity inmate
- The Big Boss holds philosophical seminars about The Matrix

## Technology/Magic
- Hypnotism is real and extremely powerful (Reggie)
- The Matrix is literally real — the red pill actually transports people
- Whales can be used as aircraft
- Beacons can be lit to summon armies
- Godzilla exists (summoned during the Ep1-2 chaos)

## Cultural References
- NFT/crypto culture (minting, selling out, floor price, degenerates)
- Andrew Tate (the Big Boss character)
- The Matrix (both as reference and literal plot device)
- George Orwell / War of the Worlds (Kyle confuses them)
- Seth Green (appears or is referenced in Utopia)
- Billionaire culture (Elon Musk buying Twitter, Bezos rocket, spending on NFTs)

## Themes
- Every attempt to succeed causes more destruction
- The line between delusion and reality is blurry (Matrix being real)
- Crypto/NFT culture as inherently chaotic and self-destructive
- Loyalty despite incompetence (Kyle and Partner's friendship)
- Internet culture (keyboard warriors, radio shock jocks, smooth brains)

## Recurring Patterns
- Kyle makes a decision → disaster follows → they flee → new disaster
- "God damn it Kyle" appears in every episode
- Reggie the hypnotist keeps getting re-hired
- Each episode ends with escalation (war → explosion → arrest → dimensional travel)
